Victoria's Clean Energy Transition Profile

An interactive data exploration tracking coal retirement, renewable generation metrics, and regional asset density.

01

Mapping the New Power Landscape

The geographic backbone of Victoria's energy grid is undergoing a massive migration. Historically anchored to the heavy fossil-fuel infrastructure concentrated in the east, the state's modern generation capacity is physically pulling outward. Look closely at how the windy coastal breezes of the Western District and the intense solar irradiance of the northern Goulburn Valley have created vibrant regional hubs, changing where our electricity is born.

Regional Decentralised Infrastructure Shifting Outward
02

Victoria's Place in the Race

How does Victoria stack up in the national race toward net-zero? While neighboring Tasmania leverages an envious natural gift of high-altitude hydro lakes to run on near-total renewables, Victoria's historical reliance on dense brown coal has made its climb much steeper. Yet, this benchmark highlights that Victoria is no longer an underachiever, successfully overtaking several states to anchor itself firmly in the progressive middle tier.

40.6% Renewable Grid Penetration Share
03

The Long Twilight of Brown Coal

For nearly a century, brown coal was the undisputed titan powering Victorian households and industries, but that era is facing a structural decline. By tracking this timeline, you can see the literal unmaking of a legacy system. Use the drop-down menu below to isolate individual energy tracks and watch the dramatic, steady drop of coal baseloads as aging plants slowly switch off to make way for cleaner alternatives.

-39.7% Reduction in Brown Coal Baseload Since 2008
04

The Anatomy of Today's Market Share

Looking at raw generation numbers can mask the true balance of power. By flattening the entire 2023-24 grid output into a single normalized percentage bar, we can see the exact proportion of market space each resource commands. Hover over the segments to see how close renewables are to claiming the majority slice of Victoria's energy profile.

100% Base Normalized Grid Proportion Share
05

The Divergence of Energy Paths

When you separate the traditional fossil fuel industry from the clean energy transition, the stark divergence between the two paths becomes completely clear. This side-by-side presentation captures a profound changing of the guard. The traditional fossil fuel sectors are steadily sloping downward, while the wind and solar trajectories display a sharp upward trend, highlighting the accelerating momentum of new energy infrastructure.

7 Assets Independent Stream Trajectories Mapped
06

The Velocity of Wind and Solar

Intermittent renewables—specifically wind and solar installations—are no longer just auxiliary players on the margins of our electrical grid. This layered stream demonstrates the velocity of their collective growth. Hover over the area track to see how their combined annual output has surged past the long-term historical average (the red target baseline), proving that alternative energy has become a primary driver of Victoria's grid.

07

Visualizing the Structural Migration

This grid shifts focus away from raw timeline curves to reveal the changing density of our power generation. By viewing generation volume as color intensity, you can watch the physical weight of Victoria's energy mix shift right before your eyes. Over the years, the deep, heavy blues that once dominated the traditional coal row slowly fade out, while the wind and solar rows light up with growing intensity, capturing the ongoing transition in a single snapshot.
